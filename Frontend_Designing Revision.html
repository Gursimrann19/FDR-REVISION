14. What is CLS,LCP and FIRST INPUT DELAY
CLS (Cumulative Layout Shift):
Measures visual stability, ensuring elements don't jump around unexpectedly during loading, with a goal of 0.1 or less.
LCP (Largest Contentful Paint):
Measures loading performance by tracking when the largest image or text block becomes visible, aiming for under 2.5 seconds for a good experience.
FID (First Input Delay):
Measures responsiveness to a user's first interaction (like a click or tap), aiming for under 100 milliseconds; it shows how quickly the page reacts. (Note: Google is shifting focus from FID to INP (Interaction to Next Paint), which tracks all interactions, not just the first one, for better overall responsiveness.)
